## Why Chickahominy and Pemberwick?

Chickahominy and Pemberwick are two neighboring communities in the northwestern part of Greenwich that share something in common: they both offer genuine neighborhood identity, engaged community life, and access to the full Greenwich school system at price points that make homeownership realistic for a wider range of buyers.

I've sold homes across Greenwich for over 40 years, and these neighborhoods deserve more attention than they typically get. Here's what buyers should know.

## Neighborhood Identity

What sets Chickahominy and Pemberwick apart from other parts of Greenwich is the strength of their community bonds:

- **Chickahominy** has its own civic association that organizes events, maintains common spaces, and advocates for the neighborhood
- **Pemberwick** has a similar community-driven culture, with active neighbors and local gathering spots
- Both areas have a "small town within a town" feel that residents prize
- Block parties, seasonal events, and neighborhood fundraisers are part of the rhythm here

In a town where many neighborhoods feel like collections of individual estates, Chickahominy and Pemberwick feel like actual communities where people know each other.

## Schools

Families in Chickahominy and Pemberwick are served by:

- **Glenville School** (K-5) — A neighborhood school with a loyal following and strong community involvement
- **Western Middle School** (6-8)
- **Greenwich High School** (9-12) — 2,700+ students, AP/IB programs

As with all Greenwich neighborhoods, the high school is shared across the entire town. A family buying a home here at $600K has the same access to Greenwich High School's AP/IB programs as a family in a $10M home elsewhere in town.

## Glenville Center

Both neighborhoods are close to **Glenville center**, a small commercial area with local shops, services, and the **Glenville Metro-North station**. It's not as bustling as downtown Greenwich or Old Greenwich's Sound Beach Avenue, but it has the essentials and a friendly, unpretentious character.

The **Glenville station** provides direct Metro-North service to Grand Central Terminal in approximately 55 minutes.

## Housing Stock

The homes in Chickahominy and Pemberwick are primarily:

- **Cape Cods and colonials** from the mid-20th century
- **Ranches and split-levels** on modest lots
- **Some newer renovations and additions** as buyers discover the value here
- Lot sizes are typically smaller than other Greenwich neighborhoods, ranging from a quarter acre to one acre

The housing stock is unpretentious and well-maintained. Many homes have been updated over the years, and the smaller lot sizes mean less yard maintenance, which appeals to busy families.

## What Homes Cost

- **Starter homes and smaller capes**: $500K-$800K
- **Updated 3-4 bedroom homes**: $800K-$1.5M
- **Larger or renovated properties**: $1.5M-$2M

These prices make Chickahominy and Pemberwick the second most affordable entry point to Greenwich after Byram, with arguably stronger neighborhood cohesion.

## Is Chickahominy/Pemberwick Right for You?

These neighborhoods are a good fit if you're looking for:

- A tight-knit community with active neighborhood involvement
- Access to Greenwich schools at moderate price points
- A neighborhood school (Glenville) with a loyal, engaged community
- Metro-North access via Glenville station
- An entry point to the Greenwich market with strong long-term value
