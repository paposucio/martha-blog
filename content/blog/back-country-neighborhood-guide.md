## Why Back Country?

Back Country Greenwich is where the town's rural, estate-style character comes to life. Sprawling properties on two to fifty-plus acres, equestrian facilities, stone walls, and mature landscaping define this area. If you're looking for privacy, land, and a pace of life that feels worlds away from Manhattan (even though it's only an hour), Back Country is it.

I've sold homes across every Greenwich neighborhood for over 40 years, and Back Country remains one of the most special areas in all of Fairfield County. Here's what buyers should know.

## North Street Corridor

**North Street** is the spine of Back Country Greenwich and home to some of the town's most prestigious addresses. This corridor runs north from the Merritt Parkway into the heart of the Back Country, lined with gated estates, horse farms, and properties that have been in families for generations.

Properties along North Street and its surrounding lanes regularly trade in the $3.5M to $15M+ range, with occasional listings above $20M for the largest parcels.

## Equestrian Properties

Back Country is the center of Greenwich's equestrian community. Many properties include:

- Riding rings and paddocks
- Multi-stall barns with heated tack rooms
- Direct access to riding trails and nature preserves
- Acreage suitable for hobby farms or serious equestrian use

The **Greenwich Riding and Trails Association** maintains a network of trails through the Back Country, connecting private properties with public land.

## Nature and Open Space

Back Country borders and includes some of Greenwich's finest conservation land:

- **Audubon Center in Greenwich** — 686 acres of trails and wildlife habitat
- Multiple land trust preserves with hiking, birding, and cross-country skiing
- Stone walls, wetlands, ponds, and woodland that give the area its timeless New England character

For buyers who value nature and privacy, this is the part of Greenwich where you'll find both in abundance.

## Schools in Back Country

Back Country families are served by:

- **North Street School** (K-5) — A smaller, community-oriented elementary school
- **Western Middle School** (6-8)
- **Greenwich High School** (9-12)

Many Back Country families also choose from Greenwich's strong private school options, including **Greenwich Academy**, **Brunswick School**, and **Greenwich Country Day School**, all of which are a short drive away.

## Commuting Considerations

Back Country is the one Greenwich neighborhood where the commute requires some planning:

- The nearest Metro-North station is **Greenwich station**, about a 10-15 minute drive
- Total commute to Grand Central Terminal is approximately 60-70 minutes door to door
- Many Back Country residents keep a second car at the station or use a car service
- Access to the **Merritt Parkway** is convenient for driving to Stamford, White Plains, or the airports

## What Homes Cost in Back Country

- **Smaller homes on 2-4 acres**: $2M-$4M
- **Mid-range estates on 4-10 acres**: $4M-$8M
- **Large estates and compounds**: $8M-$15M+

Back Country pricing reflects the land. Acreage, condition, and location along key corridors like North Street, Round Hill Road, and Lake Avenue all drive value. New construction estates with modern amenities on historic parcels are also increasingly common.

## Is Back Country Right for You?

Back Country is ideal if you're looking for:

- Privacy and acreage on multi-acre lots
- Equestrian facilities or the ability to build them
- A rural, estate-style lifestyle within reach of NYC
- Access to nature preserves and trail networks
- Some of Greenwich's most prestigious addresses
