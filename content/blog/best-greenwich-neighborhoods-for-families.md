## Choosing a Family Neighborhood in Greenwich

Greenwich has nine distinct neighborhoods, and nearly all of them work well for families thanks to the shared Greenwich High School and strong elementary schools across the district. That said, some neighborhoods stand out depending on what matters most to your family.

Here are my top recommendations after 40 years of helping families find their homes in Greenwich.

## Old Greenwich: The Classic Choice

**Price range: $1.2M-$8M+**

Old Greenwich checks almost every box for families: a walkable village center on Sound Beach Avenue, beach access at Tod's Point, top-rated Old Greenwich School (K-5), and a Metro-North station for commuting parents. The trade-off is price. Old Greenwich commands a premium, especially for move-in-ready homes near the village.

**Best for**: Families who prioritize walkability, beach lifestyle, and are comfortable in the upper price range.

## Riverside: Best School Value

**Price range: $1M-$6M+**

Riverside's **Blue Ribbon elementary school** is the headline, but the whole package is strong: a family-oriented community, Riverside Yacht Club, easy highway and train access, and generally better value per square foot than neighboring Old Greenwich. The Riverside Association keeps the community connected with events throughout the year.

**Best for**: Families who want top-tier schools and a strong community at a somewhat lower price point than Old Greenwich.

## Cos Cob: Affordable Entry Point

**Price range: $700K-$4M+**

Cos Cob offers the most affordable path into the Greenwich school system. The neighborhood has its own character (thanks to its art colony heritage), a walkable village, harbor views, and a community school that parents love. For families coming from NYC who don't want to spend $2M+, Cos Cob is where I often start the conversation.

**Best for**: First-time Greenwich buyers, young families, and anyone who wants Greenwich schools without breaking the bank.

## Mid-Country: Space and New Construction

**Price range: $1.5M-$8M+**

Mid-Country is where families go when they want land (1-5 acres), privacy, and a newer home. New construction activity has been strong here, and the central location means you're a short drive from downtown, the schools, and the train. North Mianus School (K-5) serves the area and has strong community involvement.

**Best for**: Families who want a larger property, modern floor plans, and don't mind driving to the train.

## Honorable Mentions

- **Chickahominy/Pemberwick**: Tight-knit community, Glenville School, prices starting around $500K. Great for families who value neighborhood bonds over square footage.
- **Byram**: Greenwich schools at the lowest price point in town (starting around $400K). Ideal for families focused on value and investment potential.
- **Back Country**: Estate living on multi-acre lots with equestrian options. Best for families who want privacy, land, and don't mind a longer commute.

## The Bottom Line

Every Greenwich neighborhood feeds into Greenwich High School, one of Connecticut's best. The difference comes down to elementary school preference, commute tolerance, housing style, and budget.
