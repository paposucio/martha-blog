## Why Mid-Country?

Mid-Country Greenwich occupies the geographic heart of the town, offering something that's hard to find elsewhere: spacious lots and real privacy, combined with easy access to downtown Greenwich, the highways, and the train. It's where you go when you want a 2-acre property but don't want to feel like you're living 30 minutes from civilization.

I've sold homes in Mid-Country for over 40 years, and it's become one of the most active areas for new construction in recent years. Here's what buyers should know.

## Location and Character

Mid-Country sits between downtown Greenwich and the Back Country, roughly centered around **Round Hill Road** and the surrounding lanes. The terrain is hilly, wooded, and private, with stone walls and mature trees lining the roads.

Key characteristics:

- Lots typically range from 1 to 5 acres
- A mix of classic colonials, contemporaries, and new construction
- Set back from the road with long driveways and natural screening
- A residential, quiet character without the extreme remoteness of Back Country

The area feels established but is constantly renewing. Older homes on good lots are regularly purchased and either renovated extensively or replaced with new construction.

## New Construction Activity

Mid-Country has seen the most new construction activity in Greenwich over the past several years. Builders are drawn to the combination of:

- Large lots suitable for today's open floor plans
- Central location that appeals to a wide buyer pool
- Strong resale values for quality new builds
- Proximity to downtown, schools, and the train

New construction in Mid-Country typically features modern amenities: open kitchens, home offices, finished lower levels, pools, and outdoor living spaces. Prices for new builds generally range from $3.5M to $8M+ depending on size and lot.

## Round Hill Road

**Round Hill Road** is Mid-Country's most recognized address. Running north from the Merritt Parkway, it's lined with established estates and newer builds alike. Properties on Round Hill Road and its feeder lanes carry prestige and strong long-term value.

The **Round Hill Club**, a private country club, is also located here, offering golf, tennis, swimming, and dining to its members.

## Schools in Mid-Country

Mid-Country families are served by:

- **North Mianus School** (K-5)
- **Central Middle School** (6-8)
- **Greenwich High School** (9-12)

Private school options are also convenient from Mid-Country, with **Greenwich Academy**, **Brunswick School**, and **Greenwich Country Day School** all within a 10-minute drive.

## Commuting from Mid-Country

- **Greenwich Metro-North station** is a 5-10 minute drive
- Express trains reach Grand Central Terminal in about 47 minutes
- Total door-to-door commute is typically 55-65 minutes
- **Merritt Parkway** and **I-95** are both accessible for driving

## What Homes Cost in Mid-Country

- **Older homes needing updates on 1-2 acres**: $1.5M-$2.5M
- **Updated colonials and contemporaries**: $2.5M-$5M
- **New construction on 2+ acres**: $4M-$8M+

Mid-Country offers a wide range, from homes that need work to turnkey new builds. The sweet spot for families is $2.5M-$5M for a move-in-ready home on a good-sized lot.

## Is Mid-Country Right for You?

Mid-Country is a strong fit if you're looking for:

- Privacy and space on 1-5 acre lots
- Central location with easy access to downtown and the train
- New construction options with modern floor plans
- Prestigious addresses along Round Hill Road
- A balance between Back Country seclusion and downtown convenience
